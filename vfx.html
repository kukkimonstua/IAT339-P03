The Cube

a short skit using visual effects

This is a short film done to highlight my visual effect skills and was done for Simon Fraser's Moving Images (IAT 344) course

A weird afternoon for a girl casually walking home until a glowing cube is dropped from the skies. With nothing else to do, she fumbles with it, leading to an unexpected outcome. This is a 30 second short film that was done individually in IAT 344: Moving Images class at Simon Fraser University. Through this film, I've learned how to create visual effects that include: A cube that glows, a laser beam, cloning a person, and a sci-fi portal.

Actor: Emily Chen

Storyboard

This started with an idea inspired by the movie The Avengers (2012) and the "Tesseract", which, developed into a comedic story with visual effects. The storyboard and moodboard came after to convey the tone and mood of each scene visually. The final result closely follows the storyboard but some shots were revised for the better.

Filming

I scouted out an appropriate location to shoot my film with the thought of convienience for the actor and me. This area was secluded as very little vehicles and people pass by. However, I was unable to re-shoot footage as it was raining for many weeks. Weather should be checked early on or the filming could have begun earlier next time.

As the actor was my close friend, we we're both in a comfortable environment and I was able to easily direct her through the film.â€‹ However, she was also busy with school and could only help film on specific days. Hence, I was unable to re-shoot shaky footage but used the warp stabilizer effect instead.

Visual Effects - Portal using Fractal Noise

To make the portal, I used fractal noise, turbulent displacement, and a ellipse mask to make a wavy outline. The noise rotates behind the mask by using an expression, multiplying the time it takes for one revolution and creating a distortion. Futhermore, by applying a blur, it creates a soft glow around the edges.

The turbulent displace fx is applied to give futher distortion to the outline, creating a energy field look and feel. After creating the distorted outline, I applied it to a background as a mask to make it look like it was a portal to another dimension.

Visual Effects - Glow and Clones using Rotobrush

The glow effect is applied to both the cube and beam of light. To do this, I used the roto brush tool which creates a mask around colors that are similar to the one i selected. It was quiet hard to distinguish the white box from the ground because both were of similar color. Although it took extra time to mask, the result is quite successful. As a result, I learned props should be in distinct colours for visual effects.

The same technique, rotobrushing, was applied to cloning the actor on the same screen. I also used a tri-pod to stablize the camera so that the background stayed the same. Since her clothes were darker, it was easier to apply the mask.
